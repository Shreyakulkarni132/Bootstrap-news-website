Maha Kumbh 2025

January 30, 2025 by Shreya Kulkarni

The Maha Kumbh Mela 2025 is currently underway in Prayagraj, Uttar Pradesh, from January 13 to February 26, 2025. This grand Hindu pilgrimage, held every 12 years, is renowned as the world's largest religious gathering, with an anticipated attendance of over 400 million devotees.

Significance and Rituals The festival's highlight is the ritual bathing at the Triveni Sangam, the sacred confluence of the Ganges, Yamuna, and the mythical Saraswati rivers. Devotees believe that immersing themselves in these waters purifies them of sins and aids in attaining moksha (liberation). The event also features grand processions of Naga Sadhus (ascetic warriors) and various religious ceremonies.

Lok Sabha Elections: Major Shift Expected

On February 7, 2025, the Lok Sabha was adjourned following opposition protests concerning the handcuffing of Indian deportees from the United States. Opposition members, including Congress leader Rahul Gandhi, expressed outrage over the treatment of these deportees, demanding dignified and humane handling. The protests disrupted proceedings, leading to multiple adjournments. In a recent address to the Lok Sabha, Prime Minister Narendra Modi responded to opposition criticisms, particularly those from Congress leader Rahul Gandhi. He accused opposition leaders of misleading the youth and challenging national unity, emphasizing the government's commitment to development and national security.
