Budget 2025

This is a common news landing page


Union Minister of Finance and Corporate Affairs Smt. Nirmala Sitharaman presented the Union Budget 2025-26 in Parliament today. Here is the summary of her budget speech 1. Income Tax Reforms: Increased Tax Exemption Threshold: The income tax exemption limit has been raised from ₹7 lakh to ₹12 lakh per annum, aiming to boost household consumption and savings. REUTERS.COM Revised Tax Slabs: New tax rates have been introduced for higher income brackets to simplify the tax structure. 2. Support for Agriculture: High-Yield Crop Program: A six-year mission focusing on pulses and cotton has been launched to enhance productivity and reduce import dependence. REUTERS.COM Increased Credit for Farmers: The limit for subsidized farm loans has been raised to ₹5 lakh to support farmers, fishermen, and dairy farmers. 3. Infrastructure and Capital Expenditure: Capital Spending: The government plans a modest increase in capital spending to ₹11.21 trillion in 2025-26, up from ₹10.18 trillion in the current year, focusing on infrastructure development. REUTERS.COM 4. Fiscal Deficit Target: Reduction in Fiscal Deficit: The fiscal deficit is targeted to decrease to 4.4% of GDP in 2025-26, down from a revised 4.8% in the current year, indicating a commitment to fiscal consolidation. PRSINDIA.ORG 5. Initiatives for Inclusive Development: Support for Gig Workers: Plans to provide healthcare under the PM Jan Arogya Yojana and create identity cards for gig workers have been announced. LIVEMINT.COM Focus on Women and Youth: The budget includes measures aimed at empowering women and providing opportunities for the youth. This budget reflects the government's strategy to balance economic growth with fiscal prudence, aiming to enhance the spending power of the middle class while investing in key sectors to drive long-term development.
